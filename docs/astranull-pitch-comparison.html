AstraNull comparison

AstraNull compared with IONIX and MazeBolt.

IONIX helps teams find exposed internet assets. MazeBolt tests whether DDoS defenses work. AstraNull focuses on a simpler promise: prove that the important services a customer chooses are ready, without asking for cloud keys by default.

The short pitch

AstraNull answers one plain question: if a service is attacked or bypassed, do we know what will happen, and can we prove it?

One-liner: AstraNull proves whether important services are ready for DDoS risk, without needing cloud credentials by default.

For leaders: It shows what was tested, what happened, what stayed healthy, who approved larger tests, and what changed after fixes.

For teams: Pick the service, install a small agent, run safe checks, review the proof, fix issues, and retest.

Market map

The three products solve related but different problems. IONIX finds exposed assets. MazeBolt tests DDoS defenses. AstraNull proves whether the customer's chosen services are ready.

IONIX

Find exposed internet assets

What it is good at: finding public web assets, checking web firewall coverage, finding weak or missing protection, and helping teams prioritize fixes.

What AstraNull can learn: simple labels, clear dashboards, and practical fix guidance.

MazeBolt RADAR

Test DDoS defenses

What it is good at: testing approved public targets, showing which DDoS paths get through, guiding fixes, and retesting.

What to respect: it is the mature DDoS testing benchmark.

AstraNull

Prove services are ready without default cloud access

What it is good at: testing only the services the customer chooses, using a small outbound agent for inside proof, and keeping large tests controlled.

What to protect: no default cloud keys, no surprise asset discovery, and no unmanaged attack tools.

Where we stand

AstraNull should not try to sound bigger than it is. Its strongest message is simple: safe, approved testing with proof the customer can understand.

We are stronger

Trust and control

AstraNull is designed for customers who do not want to share cloud keys. The customer chooses what to test, and large tests need approval.

We are catching up

DDoS test coverage

MazeBolt has the stronger public story for many DDoS test types. AstraNull should first win on safer proof, then grow test coverage over time.

We are different

Asset discovery

IONIX is stronger when the customer needs to find unknown assets. AstraNull starts with the services the customer has approved for testing.

How AstraNull proves it

The page should make the proof easy to follow. Every result should show what was tested, what happened outside, what the customer-side agent saw, and what to fix.

Simple talk track

Use these short answers in a customer conversation.

If they mention IONIX

"IONIX helps find what is exposed. AstraNull tests what you already care about."

IONIX is useful when the customer needs to discover unknown assets. AstraNull is useful when the customer has a known service and needs proof that protection works.

If they mention MazeBolt

"MazeBolt tests DDoS defenses. AstraNull does that with less access and tighter approval."

MazeBolt is the direct DDoS testing peer. AstraNull should stand out by requiring fewer default permissions and by showing clearer inside and outside proof.

If they ask why AstraNull

"Because proof is more than sending traffic."

The customer needs to know what was approved, what was tested, what happened, who owns the fix, and whether the fix worked. AstraNull puts that in one place.

Honest gaps and next steps

A good pitch should be believable. Here is what AstraNull should not overstate today, and what to build next.

Do not overclaim

Limits to say plainly

  • AstraNull is not production-complete yet; more staging, identity, reporting, operations, and provider work remains.
  • AstraNull should not claim MazeBolt-level DDoS test coverage until the evidence exists.
  • AstraNull should not claim IONIX-level asset discovery; broad discovery is not the core product.
  • AstraNull should not imply customers can launch large DDoS tests on their own. Approval remains required.
Build toward

Next useful proof points

  • Production-grade safe test workers across more regions.
  • Signed agent installers with tested update and rollback.
  • Approved provider workflows for larger tests, with live monitoring and stop drills.
  • Audit-ready report storage and enterprise login.
  • Optional web firewall checks that do not make cloud or firewall access mandatory.
